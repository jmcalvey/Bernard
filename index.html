Bernard "Sam" Jenaringo

Intern at Quantum Opus

Bernard Jenaringo was an intern at Quantum Opus, bringing with him a remarkable life story shaped by perseverance, curiosity, and quiet dedication.

Early Life & Background

In his late fifties, Bernard is originally from Dudsland, a small town in the Netherlands, and immigrated to the United States decades ago in search of opportunity and a better life.

Career Journey

For most of his adult years, Bernard worked humbly and tirelessly as a janitor at a local elementary school in Plymouth, Michigan. It was there that he nurtured a quiet passion for science, often reading discarded textbooks and striking up conversations with curious students and patient teachers.

His fascination with physics—particularly the quantum world—never dimmed, even as he quietly swept hallways and polished floors.

Joining Quantum Opus

Eventually, that passion led Bernard to cross paths with Quantum Opus, a Michigan-based company at the forefront of superconducting photon detection. Despite his unconventional background, his dedication and insatiable curiosity earned him a rare internship, offering him a chance to finally engage directly with the field he had admired from afar for so long.

Current Challenges

Unfortunately, Bernard is currently facing deportation proceedings by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE), placing his future in the country—and in the field he loves—in jeopardy.

Legacy of Resilience

Nevertheless, his story is one of quiet resilience: a testament to how passion can persist, and even flourish, against the odds.
